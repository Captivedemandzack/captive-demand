## How Search Engines Work and Track Results

Have you ever wondered how search engines like Google and other search engines like Bing and Yahoo determine which websites show up first when you make a query? The answer is Search Engine Optimization (SEO). SEO involves making your website more visible by optimizing it for relevant keywords or phrases so that it shows up in search engine result pages (SERPs).

Search engines use algorithms and search engine crawlers to determine the relevance of web pages to certain queries. These algorithms take into account factors such as the content on a page, its meta tags, and the number of external links pointing to the page. SEO helps to ensure that your website can be easily found by search engines, thus improving its chances of ranking higher in SERPs.

Once a website has been optimized for SEO, tracking the results can be done using tools such as Google Search Console. This tool provides insights into how people find your website through various keywords or phrases. It also gives data about user engagement and which pages are performing best. Using this information can help you optimize further for better results and identify areas where improvements can be made.

## What is SEO?

Search Engine Optimization, or “SEO” for short, is the process of improving your website to make it more visible on search engine result pages (SERPs). By optimizing your site for relevant keywords and phrases, you can improve your chances of ranking higher in SERPs and driving organic traffic to your site.

There are many different SEO strategies to increase search engine rankings, from on-page optimization to link building. On-page optimization is an important part of SEO as it helps search engines like Google understand what the page is about and how relevant it is for certain Google searches. There are many parts to search engine algorithms. They include optimizing page titles and meta descriptions, writing quality content that’s focused on targeted keywords, using internal linking to help guide users through your site, and creating an effective website structure.

Meanwhile, link building helps to increase the authority of a website by creating backlinks from external sites that point to your content. This shows search engines that other websites have found your content to be valuable and trustworthy. Link building can be done through various methods, such as guest blogging or content syndication. It’s important to focus on quality over quantity when working in link building because too many low-quality links could result in a penalty from search engines.

## Hiring a Large SEO Company vs. a Small SEO Agency

The price tag is perhaps the most significant difference between hiring a large SEO company and a small local SEO agency. ## The Downside of a Large Firm

While a comprehensive list of credentials can seem impressive, there are always a few downsides to hiring a large firm like Primelis or Neil Patel. Below are just a few:

**Price (the big one)**  
If you’re a small or medium-sized business, the chances are that you don’t have the budget to afford the big-name firms. The high price of a big company like Primelis is largely cost-prohibitive for many businesses.

**Personalization**  
When you’re one of hundreds or even thousands of clients, it isn’t easy to receive the personal attention your business deserves. You’re likely to be just another number to them, which means your goals and needs may not be given the focus they require.

**Less Effective with Your Budget**  
More prominent companies like Primelis and Neil Patel have high overheads and large teams, which means they need to charge more to cover their costs. Unfortunately, this can make them less effective with your budget, as they’ll likely require a higher retainer fee just to get started.

**Inability to Adapt and Adjust Quickly**  
Due to their size and structure, larger companies often cannot adapt and adjust quickly to changes in the market or your specific needs. This can lead to them being less effective overall, as they cannot pivot and change direction as quickly as a smaller company.

## The Benefits of Hiring a Small, Local Company

**They can focus exclusively on your business.**  
When you work with a small agency, they’ll be able to focus exclusively on your business. This means they’ll get to know your company inside and out, which will allow them to create more targeted and effective campaigns.

**They have fewer clients.**  
Since they have fewer clients, small agencies can give each one more attention. This means they’re likely to be more responsive to your needs and concerns and work harder to ensure your campaigns succeed.

**They’re more flexible.**  
Smaller agencies are usually more flexible than larger ones. This means they can be more adaptable to your changing needs and make needed changes to your campaigns on the fly.

**They’re more affordable.**  
Since small agencies don’t have the exact overhead costs as larger ones, they’re usually more affordable, which can be an excellent option for working with a tight budget.

**More personalized service**  
One of the benefits of working with a small local agency is receiving more personalized service. When you work with a larger company, you may feel like just another client. However, when you work with a smaller agency, you’ll be able to form a closer relationship with the team. This will allow them to understand your business and your specific needs better. As a result, they’ll be better equipped to create an effective SEO strategy that meets your unique goals, as well as the SEO services related to that strategy.
