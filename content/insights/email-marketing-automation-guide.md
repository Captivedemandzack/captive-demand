Most businesses treat email like a megaphone. They blast campaigns and hope something sticks. The ones that win treat it like a system. Triggers, segments, and sequences that run on autopilot. Here's the stack we use for every client.

## The Core Stack

We standardize on a few tools: Klaviyo or HubSpot for the ESP, Make or Zapier for integrations, and a CRM that actually gets used. The goal isn't more tools — it's fewer handoffs. When a lead fills out a form, they're in a nurture sequence within minutes. No manual tagging. No "I'll add them to the list tomorrow."

## Nurture vs. Campaign

Nurture sequences are evergreen. They run based on behavior: signed up, downloaded a guide, attended a webinar. Campaigns are one-offs: product launches, seasonal promos, announcements. The mistake most teams make is running everything as a campaign. The fix is to build a nurture backbone first, then layer campaigns on top.

## Measuring What Matters

Open rates are vanity. We care about revenue per subscriber, conversion from sequence steps, and unsubscribe by segment. If a segment has a 40% unsubscribe rate, the content is wrong or the audience is wrong. Fix it. The brands that treat email as a product — with clear funnels and success metrics — see 3–5x the ROI of spray-and-pray.
