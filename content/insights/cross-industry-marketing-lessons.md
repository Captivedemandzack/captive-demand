We work across verticals: SaaS, healthcare, hospitality, e-commerce, professional services. The best ideas don't stay in their lane. A med spa can steal from SaaS. A B2B brand can learn from DTC. Here's what we've learned.

## The Recurring Revenue Playbook

SaaS companies obsess over LTV, churn, and expansion revenue. Med spas and wellness brands could do the same. Membership models, subscription skincare, loyalty programs — it's the same math. The brands that treat a one-time client as a relationship, not a transaction, 3x their revenue per customer. We've seen it in both worlds.

## The Trust Problem

B2B has a long sales cycle because trust is hard. DTC has a short one because impulse buys are easy. But DTC brands that act like B2B — case studies, testimonials, clear process — convert at 2x the rate of competitors who rely on vibes. And B2B brands that borrow DTC tactics — urgency, scarcity, social proof — close faster. The lesson: steal the best of both.

## When to Ignore Your Industry

Every industry has "best practices" that are really just habits. "Healthcare doesn't do that." "SaaS buyers need 14 touchpoints." Sometimes the breakthrough is doing the opposite. We've seen med spas close high-ticket packages in one call. We've seen enterprise deals close in a week. The constraint is often mindset, not market.
